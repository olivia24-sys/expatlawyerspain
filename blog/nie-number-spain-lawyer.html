NIE Number Spain: What It Is and Do You Need a Lawyer?

ExpatLawyerSpain  ·  Immigration Guides for Expats

If you are moving to Spain, buying property, starting a business, or even just opening a bank account, you will need a NIE number. It is one of the first things every expat in Spain has to deal with - and one of the most commonly misunderstood.

What Is a NIE Number?

NIE stands for Número de Identificación de Extranjero - Foreign Identification Number. It is a unique tax identification number assigned to all non-Spanish nationals who have any kind of financial or legal dealings in Spain. Think of it as the Spanish equivalent of a National Insurance number in the UK or a Social Security number in the US.

Key fact: A NIE number is not the same as residency. You can obtain a NIE as a non-resident - for example, to complete a property purchase - without living in Spain.

When Do You Need a NIE Number?

You will need a NIE number for almost any significant transaction or legal process in Spain, including:

  • Buying or selling property
  • Opening a Spanish bank account
  • Starting or registering a business
  • Taking up employment in Spain
  • Paying taxes in Spain
  • Registering a vehicle
  • Applying for residency
  • Inheriting assets in Spain

How to Get a NIE Number in Spain

There are two main ways to obtain a NIE number:

Option 1: Apply yourself in Spain

You can apply in person at a Oficina de Extranjería (foreigners office) or at certain National Police stations. You will need to:

  • Complete form EX-15
  • Provide your passport and a photocopy
  • Provide a reason for needing the NIE (e.g. property purchase, employment)
  • Pay a small fee (around €10-€15) at a bank using form 790
  • Book an appointment in advance - waiting times vary significantly by city

In some cities, appointments can be booked weeks or even months in advance. In high-demand areas like Barcelona and Madrid, this can cause significant delays.

Option 2: Apply from outside Spain via the Spanish Consulate

If you are not yet in Spain, you can apply for a NIE at your nearest Spanish consulate. This is useful if you need the number before travelling - for example, to complete a property purchase.

Do You Need a Lawyer to Get a NIE?

No - you do not legally need a lawyer to obtain a NIE number. Many people apply successfully on their own.

However, using a lawyer to obtain your NIE is common and often worthwhile for several reasons:

  • Speed: A lawyer with existing relationships at the foreigners office can often obtain appointments faster than you can independently.
  • Convenience: You can grant a lawyer power of attorney (poder notarial) to apply on your behalf, without needing to be present. This is particularly useful if you are buying property remotely.
  • Part of a wider service: If you are using a lawyer for a property purchase or residency application, they will almost always obtain your NIE as part of the service.
  • Fewer errors: Incorrectly completed forms are a common reason for NIE applications being rejected or delayed.

NIE vs TIE vs Residency Certificate - What's the Difference?

These are three different documents that expats often confuse:

  • NIE: A tax identification number. It is just a number - not a physical card in most cases. Required for financial and legal transactions.
  • TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero): A physical residency card issued to non-EU nationals with long-term residency in Spain.
  • Certificado de Registro de Ciudadano de la UE: The residency certificate for EU citizens living in Spain. Replaced the old green residency card.

If you are a UK citizen post-Brexit, your situation is different from EU citizens. A specialist immigration lawyer can advise on the right route for your circumstances.

How Long Does It Take?

If you apply in person and your appointment goes smoothly, you can sometimes receive your NIE the same day. In practice, processing times vary by region. Using a lawyer with a power of attorney can sometimes speed up the process significantly.
