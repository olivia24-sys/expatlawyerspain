How to Find an English-Speaking Lawyer in Spain

ExpatLawyerSpain  ·  Legal Guides for Expats

Moving to Spain, buying property, or dealing with a legal issue as a foreigner can feel overwhelming - especially when you're not sure where to start. Finding a lawyer who speaks fluent English and understands the needs of expats is one of the most important steps you can take. This guide explains exactly how to do it.

Why You Need an English-Speaking Lawyer

Spanish law is complex and differs significantly from UK, US, Irish and Australian legal systems. Even if your Spanish is conversational, legal terminology is a completely different matter. A mistranslation in a property contract or residency application can have serious and costly consequences.

An English-speaking lawyer in Spain does more than translate - they bridge the gap between two legal systems and ensure you understand exactly what you are signing and agreeing to.

What to Look for in an Expat Lawyer

  • Fluent English: Not just basic English - they should be able to explain complex legal concepts clearly in your language.
  • Relevant speciality: A property lawyer is not the same as an immigration lawyer. Make sure their expertise matches your needs.
  • Registered with the Colegio de Abogados: All practising lawyers in Spain must be registered with their regional bar association.
  • Transparent fees: Reputable lawyers will give you a clear fee estimate upfront. Avoid anyone who is vague about costs.
  • Experience with foreign clients: Lawyers who regularly work with expats understand the common issues and can move faster.

Where to Find English-Speaking Lawyers in Spain

1. Use a verified directory

The easiest starting point is a directory of verified, English-speaking lawyers. ExpatLawyerSpain.com lists trusted lawyers across Spain - you can search by city and specialty, then send an enquiry directly through the platform.

2. Your country's consulate or embassy

The British Consulate, US Embassy and other national embassies in Spain maintain lists of English-speaking lawyers they have vetted. These lists are publicly available on their websites.

3. Expat community recommendations

Expat Facebook groups and forums such as r/SpainExpats are good for personal recommendations, though always verify a recommendation independently before hiring anyone.

4. Law societies

The Law Society of England and Wales maintains a directory of English solicitors who practise in Spain. Some UK-qualified solicitors are also qualified in Spanish law.

How Much Does a Lawyer Cost in Spain?

Legal fees in Spain vary widely depending on the complexity of the work and the lawyer's experience. As a rough guide:

  • Property purchase: Typically 1% of the property price, often with a minimum fee of €1,000-€1,500.
  • Immigration and residency: €500-€2,000 depending on the visa type and complexity.
  • Wills: €200-€500 for a straightforward Spanish will.
  • Employment disputes: Hourly rates of €150-€350 are common.

Always ask for a written fee agreement before any work begins.

Questions to Ask Before You Hire

  • How many expat clients have you worked with?
  • Are you registered with the local Colegio de Abogados?
  • What are your fees, and are there any additional costs?
  • How quickly can you take on my case?
  • Who will handle my case day-to-day - you or a junior?

Final Tips

Never hire a lawyer based on a single recommendation or the first result you find online. Take time to speak to at least two lawyers before committing. Most offer a free initial consultation.

And remember - a good lawyer is not an unnecessary expense. In Spain, the cost of not having proper legal advice on a property purchase or residency application can run into tens of thousands of euros. It is always worth it.
